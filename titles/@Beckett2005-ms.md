> [!abstract]
> This is a book about practitioners’ ‘hot action’. It seeks to make sense of thoseprocesses and acts of judgement endemic to everyday experiences at work andindeed in life, from which adults learn. Our focus is on paid workers, be they teachers, crane-drivers, nurses, paramedics, fast-food retailers, surgeons, gardeners, lawyers and the like, but we also believe unpaid work, in the community and in households—say, as parents—also suits this experiential emphasis.Any setting which is amenable to provoking deeper consideration of intelligent action, that is, where adults can learn how to go on, is of interest to us. Suchlearning has an epistemological basis in ‘know how’—and philosophers have arich literature in ‘knowing how’ to go on.
