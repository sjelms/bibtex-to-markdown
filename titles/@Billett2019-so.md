> Billett, Stephen. 2019. “Vocational Education and the Individual.” [[John Wiley and Sons]]. https://onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/10.1002/9781119098713.ch3

> [!abstract]
> Understanding the purposes, processes, and outcomes of vocational education requires placing individuals at the centre of analysis. The development of national vocational education systems—their structures, goals, and practices—is increasingly shaped by institutional factors, which John Dewey (1916) describes as the “social side.” These include prescribed learning outcomes, curriculum content, and assessment frameworks. However, the effectiveness and continuity of these systems depend on how individuals engage with, interpret, and reshape them—what Dewey terms the “personal side.” These two dimensions are mutually dependent. Whether oriented toward practical outcomes or educational effectiveness, vocational education relies on individuals’ active participation and experience. Individuals ultimately enact vocational education provisions, learn through them, contribute to their adaptation, and realise their intended purposes.
