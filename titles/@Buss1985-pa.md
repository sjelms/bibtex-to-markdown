> [!bibliography]
> Buss, Dale D. 1985. "Winners And Losers: On the factory floor, technology brings challenge for some, drudgery for others." [[Wall Street Journal Eastern Ed]].

> [!abstract]
> This article examines the impact of advanced manufacturing technologies on workers in the U.S. automotive industry during the mid-1980s. Drawing on examples from Ford and General Motors plants, Buss argues that the technological transformation of factory production creates both opportunities and challenges for workers. Automation and computer-integrated manufacturing increase demand for multiskilled technicians, require extensive retraining, and can provide workers with greater responsibility, autonomy, and career prospects. At the same time, some employees struggle to adapt to new technical requirements, resist the loss of traditional craft practices, or find that automation reduces their jobs to routine monitoring tasks. The article highlights the uneven consequences of technological change, showing how the same innovations can simultaneously produce upskilling, enhanced work satisfaction, deskilling, and increased workplace monotony. Buss concludes that the successful implementation of new technologies depends not only on technical systems but also on the ability of firms and workers to adapt through continuous learning and training.
