> [!bibliography]
> Fuller, Alison, Lorna Unwin. 2014. "A Sense of Belonging: The Relationship Between Community and Apprenticeship." [[Routledge]]. https://www.taylorfrancis.com/chapters/edit/10.4324/9781315042091-15

> [!abstract]
> In this chapter, we share our formative ideas about the relationship between apprenticeship and a four-dimensional concept of community. We will explore the extent to which community remains relevant to contemporary forms of apprenticeship, most notably the Modern Apprenticeship in the UK. We argue that, given the increasingly fractured nature of social and occupational relationships, it is necessary to intervene and actively reconstruct some semblance of those communal experiences from which apprentices benefited in the past. Constructing and implementing a modern model of apprenticeship under contemporary economic, social, and cultural conditions presents a considerable challenge for policymakers, employers, and vocational education and training (VET) practitioners. This chapter draws on data published by the Department for Education and Employment in the UK, which show that significant numbers of young people are leaving the Modern Apprenticeship before completing the program (see DfEE, 1998a), against a background of continued concern that too many UK employers are unwilling to make a substantial investment in improving their workforces' skills (see Keep and Mayhew, 1995, 1996). Finally, we discuss ways in which both early leaver rates and employer ambivalence create a strong case for intervention in the Modern Apprenticeship to build support infrastructures that recreate the positive aspects of past communal structures.
