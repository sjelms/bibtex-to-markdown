> Charles A.E. Goodhart's book Monetary Theory and Practice, The U.K. Experience contains a collection of papers written between 1968 and 1983 while the author served as specialist adviser in monetary economics at the Bank of England. The book makes interesting reading for a number of reasons. First, Goodhart's importance at the Bank increased through the years so that by the end of the period he was one of the chief advisers at the Bank. Second, very few economists have stayed with a major central bank for as long a period while continuing to write on technical aspects of monetary policy. Third, the macroeconomic and monetary disturbances of the period provided interesting problems that the Bank had to deal with. On the academic side too, the Bank's monetary adviser had to consider the relevance of monetarism for making policy. Finally, for those familiar only with the American scene, Goodhart's Bank of England perspective on all this is useful.
