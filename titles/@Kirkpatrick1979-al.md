> [!bibliography]
> Kirkpatrick, Donald L. 1979. "Techniques for Evaluating Training Programs." Vol. 33. [[Association for Talent Development]].

> [!abstract]
> A group of 4 studies has as one of its purposes to stimulate training directors to take a penetrating look at the process of evaluation, since the future of training programs depends to a large extent on the ability to use and evaluate results. A second objective is to suggest procedures and methods for such training program evaluation. Three phases of evaluation are presented: 1. evaluation in terms of results, 2. evaluation in terms of learning, and 3. evaluation in terms of on-the-job behavior. Some of the problems and approaches to measuring training programs in terms of final objective-results are analyzed. It appears that the evaluation of training programs in terms of results is progressing at a rather slow rate, and at the present time, research techniques are not adequate.
