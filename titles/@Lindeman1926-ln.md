> [!abstract]
> The Meaning of Education by Eduard C. Lindeman is a thought-provoking exploration of the purpose and nature of education, emphasizing lifelong learning, experience-based education, and the social role of education in democracy. Published in the early 20th century, Lindeman’s work challenges traditional notions of schooling. A key influence in the field of adult education, Lindeman’s ideas laid the foundation for modern concepts of experiential learning and informal education. The Meaning of Education remains a significant work for educators, policymakers, and lifelong learners who seek a deeper understanding of education’s transformative potential.
