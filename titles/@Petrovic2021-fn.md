> This chapter discusses the latter, and this chapter looks into the forms of the apartments in those parts of the world where apartments are commonly used. In order to track the reasons for modern appreciations for apartment living, it is useful to review the ideas associated with the emergence of the apartments during the second half of the 19th century, around the times of industrialisation and urbanisation. The bourgeois apartment buildings of 19th-century Paris organised the previous jumble of rooms within the maison à allée into a clear and practical system. London’s apartment journey was very different to that of Paris or Berlin. Despite being the centre of the British Empire and the world’s first city to surpass 1million residents by 1811, London did grow upwards as much as it expanded outwards into opulent suburbs, leaving the city centre for those too poor to move.
