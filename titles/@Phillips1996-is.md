> [!abstract]
> The return on investment (ROI) on training of employees needs to be computed because this provides solid evidence that particular courses are achieving their targeted results. Therefore, results have to be converted to monetary values and compared with the cost of the program to convince management that employee training is doing what it is supposed to do. The steps involved in converting data to monetary values are focusing on a single unit, finding a value for each unit, computing the change in performance, obtaining an annual amount and determining the annual value. However, this is not the only way to convert data to monetary values. Training professionals can also convert output to contribution, compute the cost of quality, convert employees' time, utilize historic costs, employ internal and external experts, make use of data from external studies, or use estimates of participants, supervisors, senior managers and human resources.
