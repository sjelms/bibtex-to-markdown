> [!abstract]
> Robert B. Reich's The Work of Nations argues that globalization has redefined national wealth, shifting its foundation from the success of domestic corporations to the skills and adaptability of its people. He categorizes the modern workforce into three groups: high-value Symbolic-Analytic workers, vulnerable Routine laborers facing global competition, and low-wage In-Person service providers. Because capital is now borderless, national policy must change focus from protecting industries to investing in human potential. To prevent severe economic stratification, Reich proposes a multi-pronged policy solution: reforming tax codes for greater wealth contribution, making massive public investments in education and infrastructure, and embracing open trade rather than protectionism.
