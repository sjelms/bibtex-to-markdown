> [!abstract]
> The new welfare labour process in this research was designed with austerity in mind. Quite apart from either direct cuts in welfare rates or the outsourcing of design work to the private sector, to firmly secure its interests the state sought to discipline the poor by disciplining those charged with administering their rights. It did this with tried and tested methods: through radical labour process re-design. Having reviewed the materials thus far, we see better now how changing the way workers worked – channelling the investment of talents, learning and energy, and securing consent – became central to the needs of the capitalist state. However, not all workers became invested in the dynamics of administrative knowledge production that the complex mediations of this new labour process design recommended. The practices, skills and knowledge of consenting sometimes went unlearned.
