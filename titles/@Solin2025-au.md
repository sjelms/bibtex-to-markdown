> Solin, Isabel, Laura Gutierrez Bucheli, Duncan W Maxwell. 2025. “A global review of Modern Methods of Construction (MMC) workforce training.” [[The Architectural Science Association ANZAScA]]. http://dx.doi.org/10.65388/tmif5530

> [!abstract]
> The global construction industry has faced a persistent productivity crisis. Traditional construction methods appear insufficient for significant improvement, requiring a paradigm shift towards Modern Methods of Construction (MMC). By leveraging innovation and off-site manufacturing, MMC offers an alternative for greater project certainty and efficiency while diversifying the workforce. Though MMC’s technical, product- focused aspects have been explored in academia and the industry, the specific workforce skills and training required for effective implementation remain under-explored and undefined. This paper explores this gap through a global market review of existing MMC skills-training programs. The review analyses global MMC program integration typologies, training types and characteristics. Key findings reveal varied training practices and approaches internationally. From this analysis, three distinct typologies of national MMC workforce development emerged: centralised (United Kingdom), specialist (Singapore), and fragmented (United States). Lastly, New Zealand is discussed for its emergent approach, which offers valuable lessons for other countries because of recent leadership changes within its vocational education system present a cautionary signal regarding future stability and progress.
