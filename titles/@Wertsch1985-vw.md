> [!abstract]
> According to Vygotsky’s general genetic law of cultural development, all higher mental functions appear first on the interpsychological plane and then on the intrapsychological plane. This claim is central to Vygotsky’s approach, but the processes it entails have not yet been examined in any great detail. Vygotsky was most specific about it in connection with his account of egocentric and inner speech, where he analyzed the forms of semiotic mediation that make possible the transition from interpsychological to intrapsychological functioning. In that analysis, however, he focused almost exclusively on the latter. My concern here is with properties of interpsychological processes
